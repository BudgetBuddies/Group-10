Preparation and testing of stream materials

The third quarter is over. As a new quarter comes in, an improved Budget Buddies welcomes itself. An upgraded version of Budget Buddies was developed using all the analytics obtained from the previous stream, notably from the preceding quarter. The team openly acknowledged all of the criticism and made changes in accordance with the feedback from the audience. In light of this, the following improvements have been made in order to better prepare for and present the second upcoming stream:

Promotional Materials

The team produced numerous promotional materials to advertise our upcoming live stream, Budget Buddies Episode II: HUSTLE! To increase depth and enthusiasm in our second promotional campaign, we developed new and enhanced posters and a promotional video that include our streaming date, time, and content. To stick to our brand theme, a similar color palette was used in these designs. However, the foundation of these designs greatly differs from our first promotional campaign. While our initial material used wavy elements, these modernized posters used geometric elements, designed to guide the eyes of viewers to the areas of the material which we want them to see. Additionally, the team implemented a raffle to hype up the stream and gain a larger audience. For a better chance of reaching more people, the streamers also shared these materials individually. Streamlabs Testing

In preparation for our second stream, we thoroughly tested every component of our streaming software to avoid any unprecedented technical issues. Aspects such as stream alerts, cameras, chatbox, and audio levels were tested once they were integrated and linked into our stream layout through the software.

Dry Run

In addition to setting up and testing the stream components, the streamers also conducted a dry run to simulate the upcoming live stream. This assisted us in familiarizing ourselves with our outline and the manner in which we will conduct the actual stream. The crew was able to develop backup plans for potential technological issues and complete everything before the stream by repeatedly practicing and acknowledging individual roles.
